If monster repellants were Lays flavors‍‍

Flamin' Hot Dill Pickle Fried Pickles we think theyre a big dill. Lays Flamin Hot Dill Pickle Crusted Pickles Recipe Ingredients: 1 jar Thick-cut dill pickle chips or spears 1 bag Lays Flamin Hot Dill Pickle flavored potato chips 2 Eggs + 1 Tbsp. Water 1 cup All-Purpose flour cup Corn starch 1 tsp. Kosher salt 1 tsp. Garlic powder tsp. Cayenne pepper tsp. Black pepper Instructions: Pre-heat oven to 375 F. Drain dill pickle chips or spears from pickle juice. Lightly pat pickles dry with a paper towel. Crush the Lays Flamin Hot dill Pickle chips, and pour into a shallow tray or plate. In a bowl, make egg-wash by whisking together the eggs and water. Make seasoned flour by mixing the flour and cornstarch with all spices and seasonings. Place into a shallow tray or plate. Bread the dill pickles by lightly tossing in the seasoned flour. Shake off the excess, then dip into the egg-wash. Finally, coat the pickles with the crushed Lays, gently pressing the chip pieces to adhere to the pickles. Transfer the crusted pickles onto a sheet tray lined with parchment paper. Bake for approximately 15 minutes, or until the coating is crunchy, but chips are not burnt. Allow to cool slightly before serving alongside your favorite dipping sauce!

Chip or treat! Get into spooky SZN by making these Lays chocolate-covered ghoulish treats. ‍ Recipe below.  Ingredients: One bag of Lays Wavy chips cup of milk chocolate chips cup of espresso chips cup of white chocolate Coconut oil Edible candy eyes Mixed sprinkles Colored food dye Recipe: Place cup of milk chocolate chips with tablespoon of coconut oil in a microwave safe bowl. Heat in the microwave on 50% power level for 30 seconds until chocolate is melted. Do the same with the espresso chips and white chocolate, until all chocolate is melted. Add preferred food dye color to white chocolate and mix. Dip Lays Wavy chips into chocolate as desired and lay on parchment paper to dry (espresso chocolate for bats, milk chocolate for monsters). Drizzle colored white chocolate on top to add some flare. Top with edible eyes and sprinkles to make the bats and monsters. Place in freezer for at least 30 minutes, until chocolate dries. Enjoy!

Absolutely breathtaking (the mountains/lake/trees/sky are alright too).
